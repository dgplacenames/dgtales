Galloway and Dumfriesshire Tales and Traditions

The Laird of Knocksheen

From off a hill whose concave womb reworded
A plaintful story from a sistering vale,
My spirits to attend this double voice accorded,
And down I laid to list the sad-tuned tale;
Ere long espied a fickle maid full pale,
Tearing of papers, breaking rings a-twain,

Source

  • Malcolm M'Lachlan Harper, Rambles in Galloway: Topgraphical, Historical, Traditional, and Biographical (1876), pp. 166-167

Notes

  • The two local writers Harper mentions are presumably Robert Trotter and John Gordon Barbour.
